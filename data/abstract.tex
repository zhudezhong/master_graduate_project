\begin{cabstract}
随着互联网与移动终端的快速发展，图像、文本等多媒体数据规模持续增长，如何在海量数据中实现高效准确的相似检索已成为信息检索领域中的重要研究问题。哈希检索通过将高维特征映射为紧凑的二进制编码，并利用汉明距离进行近似最近邻搜索，在存储开销和检索效率方面具有明显优势，因此被广泛应用于大规模多媒体检索任务。然而，现有多数哈希方法通常基于静态数据集进行离线训练，假设数据分布在整个检索过程中保持不变。
在实际应用环境中，数据往往以流式形式持续到达，概念漂移不可避免的会产生，即新的类别不断出现，已有数据的分布也可能随时间发生变化。
当概念漂移发生时，模型原有学习到的语义关系难以长期保持，从而导致检索性能逐渐下降。此外，一些动态哈希方法需要重新对历史样本进行重新编码，计算和维护开销较大，难以满足在线检索系统的实时更新需求。因此，如何在数据持续到达的条件下实现稳定且高效的在线哈希检索，成为当前研究中的一个重要问题。
围绕概念漂移环境下的动态哈希检索问题，本文从单模态图像检索、跨模态检索以及系统应用三个方面开展研究。

在动态单模态图像检索任务中，本文提出一种半监督概念保留哈希方法（Semi-supervised Concept Preserving Hashing，SCPH）。该方法通过为不同类别构建固定的概念编码，使类别语义在汉明空间中具有稳定的语义锚点，从而在数据不断更新的情况下保持语义结构的一致性。同时，为缓解标注数据不足带来的影响，SCPH利用样本之间的K近邻关系为未标注样本生成伪标签，并与已有标签共同参与模型训练。在模型学习过程中，通过联合约束样本相似性保留、目标编码一致性以及哈希编码均衡性，使新到达数据的语义关系能够有效映射到汉明空间。该方法在模型更新过程中无需重新计算历史样本的哈希码，从而在控制系统计算开销的同时提高了模型在概念漂移环境下的鲁棒性与检索稳定性。

在动态跨模态检索任务中，本文进一步提出一种基于多哈希表结构的增量式跨模态哈希方法（Multi-table based Incremental Hashing，MIH）。该方法通过构建多个哈希表来保存不同时期学习得到的跨模态语义映射，使模型在持续更新过程中能够保留历史语义信息，从而缓解新数据学习对已有语义结构的影响。同时，根据各哈希表在新数据上的表现对其贡献进行动态调整，并在检索阶段结合查询样本与哈希超平面的关系，对不同哈希比特位进行自适应加权，以获得更为细粒度的加权汉明距离度量。通过上述策略，模型能够在适应数据分布变化的同时保持历史语义结构，从而提高概念漂移环境下跨模态检索的准确性。

在算法研究基础上，本文进一步面向电商应用场景设计并实现了一个基于动态哈希检索的智能检索系统。系统以SCPH与MIH为核心算法模块，构建了包括数据接入、特征提取、哈希编码、索引存储与检索服务在内的完整处理流程，实现了相似商品推荐、拍照购以及自然语言搜索等典型检索功能。系统测试结果表明，在不重新编码历史哈希码的情况下，所提出的方法能够支持新数据的持续接入与在线更新，并保持稳定的检索效果。

实验部分中，本文在CIFAR-10、CIFAR-100、MIRFlickr和MSCOCO数据集上分别构建了新类别出现和数据分布变化两类概念漂移场景，并与多种静态及动态哈希方法进行了比较。实验结果表明，在多时间步的检索过程中，本文提出的SCPH与MIH能够保持较为稳定的检索性能，并在概念漂移发生后表现出更好的适应能力。消融实验进一步验证了概念编码约束、多哈希表结构以及查询自适应加权等关键模块在性能提升中的作用。

综上，本文针对概念漂移环境下的动态哈希检索问题提出了两种有效方法，并通过电商智能检索系统验证了相关方法在实际应用中的可行性。相关研究为动态数据环境中的哈希检索提供了一种可行的解决思路，对构建稳定高效的智能检索系统具有一定参考价值。

\end{cabstract}

\begin{eabstract}
With the rapid development of the Internet and mobile devices, the scale of multimedia data such as images and texts has grown continuously. How to achieve efficient and accurate similarity retrieval from massive data has become an important research problem in the field of information retrieval. Hashing-based retrieval maps high-dimensional features into compact binary codes and performs approximate nearest neighbor search using Hamming distance. Due to its advantages in storage efficiency and retrieval speed, hashing has been widely applied to large-scale multimedia retrieval tasks. However, most existing hashing methods are trained offline on static datasets and assume that the data distribution remains unchanged during the retrieval process.
In real-world applications, data often arrive continuously in a streaming manner, and concept drift inevitably occurs. New categories may emerge, and the distribution of existing data may also change over time. When concept drift occurs, the semantic relationships learned by the model may no longer remain valid, leading to a gradual degradation in retrieval performance. In addition, some online hashing methods require re-encoding historical samples during model updates, resulting in considerable computational and maintenance costs, which makes them difficult to apply in online retrieval systems with real-time requirements. Therefore, how to achieve stable and efficient online hashing retrieval under continuously arriving data has become an important research issue.
To address the problem of online hashing retrieval under concept drift, this work conducts research from three aspects: single-modal image retrieval, cross-modal retrieval, and system implementation.

For online single-modal image retrieval, this thesis proposes a Semi-supervised Concept Preserving Hashing method (SCPH). The method assigns a fixed concept code to each category, enabling category semantics to form stable semantic anchors in the Hamming space, thereby maintaining the consistency of semantic structures as new data continuously arrive. To alleviate the problem of insufficient labeled data, SCPH generates pseudo-labels for unlabeled samples based on K-nearest neighbor relationships and incorporates them together with labeled data into the training process. During model learning, similarity preservation, target code consistency, and hash code balance are jointly optimized so that the semantic relationships of newly arriving samples can be effectively mapped into the Hamming space. The proposed method does not require re-computation of hash codes for historical samples during model updates, which reduces computational overhead while improving robustness and retrieval stability under concept drift.

For online cross-modal retrieval, this thesis further proposes a Multi-table based Incremental Hashing method (MIH). The method constructs multiple hash tables to preserve cross-modal semantic mappings learned at different time periods, allowing historical semantic information to be retained during continuous model updates and alleviating the influence of newly arriving data on previously learned semantic structures. In addition, the contributions of different hash tables are dynamically adjusted according to their performance on new data. During retrieval, the relationship between the query sample and hash hyperplanes is utilized to adaptively weight different hash bits, resulting in a finer-grained weighted Hamming distance metric. Through these strategies, the model can adapt to changes in data distribution while preserving historical semantic structures, thereby improving the accuracy of cross-modal retrieval under concept drift.

Based on the proposed algorithms, this thesis further designs and implements an intelligent retrieval system for e-commerce applications based on online hashing retrieval. The system adopts SCPH and MIH as core algorithm modules and constructs a complete processing pipeline including data ingestion, feature extraction, hash encoding, index storage, and retrieval services. It supports typical retrieval functions such as similar product recommendation, image-based product search, and natural language search. System evaluation results show that the proposed methods can support continuous data insertion and online model updating without re-encoding historical hash codes, while maintaining stable retrieval performance.

In the experimental evaluation, two typical concept drift scenarios, including the emergence of new categories and changes in data distribution, are constructed on the CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, MIRFlickr, and MSCOCO datasets. The proposed methods are compared with several static and online hashing approaches. Experimental results demonstrate that SCPH and MIH maintain more stable retrieval performance across multiple time steps and exhibit better adaptability after concept drift occurs. Ablation studies further verify the effectiveness of key components, including concept code constraints, the multi-table structure, and query-adaptive weighting, in improving retrieval performance.

In summary, this thesis proposes two effective methods for online hashing retrieval under concept drift and verifies their practical feasibility through the implementation of an intelligent e-commerce retrieval system. The proposed research provides a feasible solution for hashing retrieval in online data environments and offers useful insights for building stable and efficient intelligent retrieval systems.

\end{eabstract}
